Here are some things I would do if I were teaching a class in this situation.

First of all I really think synchronous classes are the way to go when class is online. The amount of student questions and banter with the professor is already lacking when the classes aren't face to face, but that is gone when the class is asynchronous. I do think the synchronous labs were nice, however one major issue I faced was that if a group member wasn't participating or hadn't watched the lectures, it was really difficult. I think a good middle ground would be tuesday lectures and thursday labs or something like that. I think Dr. Lam has mastered the art of online class, and his compilers course, which you are familliar with, has been the best course I've taken online, and is a good model for the lecture/lab structure. Another thing which could really benefit future courses is a canvas page. There were many occaisions where I simply forgot the blog post as it wasn't on my canvas calendar, which is the main source of orgainization in my life. Now this is mostly on me, however it would have been nice. I do however think using slack was a great idea.

Overall I did enjoy the content of this course, and think the assignments were beneficial to our learning. Also I througholy enjoyed you as a professor, and think you are quite charming and funny.
